Starbucks Capstone Challenge¶

Introduction¶

This data set contains simulated data that mimics customer behavior on the Starbucks rewards mobile app. Once every few days, Starbucks sends out an offer to users of the mobile app. An offer can be merely an advertisement for a drink or an actual offer such as a discount or BOGO (buy one get one free). Some users might not receive any offer during certain weeks.

Not all users receive the same offer, and that is the challenge to solve with this data set.

Your task is to combine transaction, demographic and offer data to determine which demographic groups respond best to which offer type. This data set is a simplified version of the real Starbucks app because the underlying simulator only has one product whereas Starbucks actually sells dozens of products.

Every offer has a validity period before the offer expires. As an example, a BOGO offer might be valid for only 5 days. You'll see in the data set that informational offers have a validity period even though these ads are merely providing information about a product; for example, if an informational offer has 7 days of validity, you can assume the customer is feeling the influence of the offer for 7 days after receiving the advertisement.

You'll be given transactional data showing user purchases made on the app including the timestamp of purchase and the amount of money spent on a purchase. This transactional data also has a record for each offer that a user receives as well as a record for when a user actually views the offer. There are also records for when a user completes an offer.

Keep in mind as well that someone using the app might make a purchase through the app without having received an offer or seen an offer.

Example¶

To give an example, a user could receive a discount offer buy 10 dollars get 2 off on Monday. The offer is valid for 10 days from receipt. If the customer accumulates at least 10 dollars in purchases during the validity period, the customer completes the offer.

However, there are a few things to watch out for in this data set. Customers do not opt into the offers that they receive; in other words, a user can receive an offer, never actually view the offer, and still complete the offer. For example, a user might receive the "buy 10 dollars get 2 dollars off offer", but the user never opens the offer during the 10 day validity period. The customer spends 15 dollars during those ten days. There will be an offer completion record in the data set; however, the customer was not influenced by the offer because the customer never viewed the offer.

Cleaning¶

This makes data cleaning especially important and tricky.

You'll also want to take into account that some demographic groups will make purchases even if they don't receive an offer. From a business perspective, if a customer is going to make a 10 dollar purchase without an offer anyway, you wouldn't want to send a buy 10 dollars get 2 dollars off offer. You'll want to try to assess what a certain demographic group will buy when not receiving any offers.

Final Advice¶

Because this is a capstone project, you are free to analyze the data any way you see fit. For example, you could build a machine learning model that predicts how much someone will spend based on demographics and offer type. Or you could build a model that predicts whether or not someone will respond to an offer. Or, you don't need to build a machine learning model at all. You could develop a set of heuristics that determine what offer you should send to each customer (i.e., 75 percent of women customers who were 35 years old responded to offer A vs 40 percent from the same demographic to offer B, so send offer A).

Data Sets¶

The data is contained in three files:

  • portfolio.json - containing offer ids and meta data about each offer (duration, type, etc.)
  • profile.json - demographic data for each customer
  • transcript.json - records for transactions, offers received, offers viewed, and offers completed

Here is the schema and explanation of each variable in the files:

portfolio.json

  • id (string) - offer id
  • offer_type (string) - type of offer ie BOGO, discount, informational
  • difficulty (int) - minimum required spend to complete an offer
  • reward (int) - reward given for completing an offer
  • duration (int) - time for offer to be open, in days
  • channels (list of strings)

profile.json

  • age (int) - age of the customer
  • became_member_on (int) - date when customer created an app account
  • gender (str) - gender of the customer (note some entries contain 'O' for other rather than M or F)
  • id (str) - customer id
  • income (float) - customer's income

transcript.json

  • event (str) - record description (ie transaction, offer received, offer viewed, etc.)
  • person (str) - customer id
  • time (int) - time in hours since start of test. The data begins at time t=0
  • value - (dict of strings) - either an offer id or transaction amount depending on the record

def clean_data( profile, transcript):
    
    # remove outliers and missing values
    profile = profile[profile['age'] != 118].reset_index(drop=True)
    
    # divide transcript into 1) "offer_received_viewed", 2) "offer_completed" and  3) "offer_in_transaction" data sets.
    offer_received_and_viewed = transcript[transcript.event.isin(['offer received', 'offer viewed'])]
    offer_completed = transcript[transcript.event == 'offer completed']    
    offer_in_transaction = transcript[transcript.event == 'transaction']
    
    # Extract "offerid" from "value" for events "offer recieved", "offer viewed" and "offer completed"
    # convert into string type. 
    offer_received_and_viewed.loc[:, 'offer_id'] = offer_received_and_viewed.loc[:, 
                                                                                 'value'].apply(lambda x: x['offer id'])
    offer_completed.loc[:, 'offer_id'] = offer_completed.loc[:, 'value'].apply(lambda x: x['offer_id'])

    # the value (amount) for these data set will be zero as there is no amount provided for these events. 
    offer_received_and_viewed['value'] = 0
    offer_completed['value'] = 0

    # extract value (amount) for in transaction
    offer_in_transaction.loc[:, 'value'] = offer_in_transaction.loc[:, 'value'].apply(lambda x:
                                                                                      x['amount']).astype('int64')

    
    # combine all the above three data sets
    transcript = pd.concat([offer_received_and_viewed, offer_completed, offer_in_transaction])
    transcript.sort_index(inplace=True)
    
    
    return profile, transcript

# create user-item matrix from profile
def create_customer_matrix(df1=transcript, df2=profile, df3=portfolio):
    '''return all the transcripts for a customer
    INPUT:
        df1: transcripts data set
        df2: profile data set
        df3: portfolio data set
    
    OUTPUT:
        customer_data: user-item matrix having customer details in rows and offers in columns 
    '''
    # get list of person id's from transcript and create customer_id column in customer_data data set
    customer_data = pd.DataFrame(df1.person.unique().tolist(), columns=['customer_id'])

# create function to get list of similar customers for a specific customer using customer_id
def find_similar_users(user_id, user_item=customer_data_2):
    '''
    INPUT:
    user_id - (int) a user_id
    user_item - (pandas dataframe) matrix of users by offer: 
                1's when a user has interacted with an offer, 0 otherwise
    
    OUTPUT:
    similar_users - (list) an ordered list where the closest users (largest dot product users)
                    are listed first
    
    Description:
    Computes the similarity of every pair of users based on the dot product
    Returns an ordered
    
    '''

    # compute similarity of each user to the provided user
    similarity = user_item.dot(user_item.loc[user_id])

    # remove non similar users
    similarity = similarity[similarity > 0]

    # sort by similarity
    similarity = similarity.sort_values(ascending=False)

    # create list of just the ids
    most_similar_users = similarity.index.tolist()
    

    # remove the own user's id
    if len(most_similar_users):
        most_similar_users.remove(user_id)
        return most_similar_users # return a list of the similar users from most to least similar

def user_user_recomendation(user1, user2, top_n=5, user_item=customer_data_2):
    '''
    INPUT:
    user1 - (int) customer_id of targeted customer
    user2 - (int) customer_id of similar/other customer
    top_n - (int) number of recommendations to make
            default value is 5
    user_item - matrix of users and offers: 
                1's when a user has influnced by (completed) an offers, 0 not influenced/completed an offer
    
    OUTPUT:
    recs - (list) list of offers that the user2 has influenced by but user1 has not.
    
    
    Description:
    compare the offers of user1 with offers of user2 and recommend the offers that are additional in user2
    return list of the offers customer has influenced by
    '''
    
    # get list of offers for the user1
    user1_offers = get_user_offers(user1, user_item)
    
    # get list of offers for the user2
    user2_offers = get_user_offers(user2, user_item)
    user2_offers = set(user2_offers)
    
    # get the list of offers that are in user2_offers but not in user1_offers
    recs = list(user2_offers.difference(user1_offers))
        
    return recs

Interesting and difficult parts¶
    

  • The were some parts I found interesting and difficult:
      

    • "User-User Based Collaborative Filtering model" could not handle the cold start problem. That's why I choose to recommend offers using "Rank-Based Recommendations" where "User-User Based Collaborative Filtering model" could not handle. And combining these two models became our "hybrid-model". 
      • 

    • Handling the ignore offers (the offers automatically completed but never viewed by the customer). As we should not recommend the these offers to a customer if he has completed the offers with out viewing these offers. I need to handle the ignored offers in "User-User Based Collaborative Filtering model" and in "hybrid-model".
      • 

    • Model evaluation part very intresting and difficult as well of the project. As I could not use the sklearn.metrics.mean_absolute_error because in the above recommendation models we are recommending new offers that the customer may find interesting. So, the recommended offers meant to be different from the offers initially completed by the customers as provided in the app data. That's why for calculating the MAE I considered the recommended offer as error if it was completed by the customer already or the recommened offer was ignored by the customer already.
      •
